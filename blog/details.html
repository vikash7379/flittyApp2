Exclusive community is the next big thing in India

India is a land of diversity where people of different cultures and religions live together in harmony. Though the country is known for its democratic values, there is a certain fascination with members-only clubs. These are places where you are waited on hand and foot, where luxury is the norm and exclusivity is so predictable that the very expectation is passé. With beautiful surroundings, snazzy amenities, and a chance to hobnob with the who’s who of the business, political, sports, and entertainment worlds, it’s not surprising that the waiting lists to get in are long and memberships need to be earned. Here’s a look at some of the best private members clubs in India. Welcome to the inner sanctum.

In India, the concept of private members' clubs is booming. With a rapidly growing economy and a burgeoning middle class, there is a new demand for luxury goods and experiences. And membership-based clubs are seen as the perfect way to access these.

Private members clubs are popping up all over India, in cities like Mumbai, Delhi, Bengaluru, and Chennai. And they come with all the trappings of luxury – beautiful surroundings, top-notch amenities, and a chance to rub shoulders with the who’s who of the business, political, sports, and entertainment worlds.

If you’re looking for a taste of the high life, here’s a look at some of the best private members clubs in India. Welcome to the inner sanctum. Soho House Mumbai is a private members club that offers a luxurious and exclusive experience to its members. Located in the heart of Mumbai's trendy Lower Parel neighbourhood, the club occupies a restored heritage building and features a range of amenities and services.

Soho House, Mumbai

One of the main attractions of Soho House Mumbai is its beautiful surroundings. The club features a rooftop pool with panoramic views of the city, as well as a spa and a gym. There is also a variety of dining options, including a rooftop bar and a restaurant serving Indian and international cuisine. In addition to its physical amenities, Soho House Mumbai also offers a range of social and cultural events for its members. These can include concerts, film screenings, and art exhibitions, as well as networking events and business talks.

The Quorum , Gurugram

The Quorum is a private members club in Gurugram, India that was founded by Vivek Narain and Sonya Jehan. It occupies 22,000 square feet and offers a range of luxurious amenities and services, including a members' lounge, a business quarter, a library, event rooms, a fitness studio that can also be used as a screening room, a gym, a shop with curated brands, a conference room, a café, a restaurant, and a bar. The club also has a space called The Alley that hosts art exhibitions and other events, and it has a full calendar of programs for its members. The Quorum has six membership categories, and applicants are required to have recommendations from existing members.

The Indus, Mumbai

The Indus Club is a private members club in Mumbai that caters to the city's wealthy elite. It offers a range of luxurious amenities and services, including dining options, recreational facilities, and event spaces. The Indus Club is known for its exclusivity, with memberships limited to those with net worths in the tens of thousands of crores, according to a club employee. In recent years, a number of exclusive clubs and networks have emerged in India, such as The A and the Wodehouse Investment Network (WIN), which offer similar experiences of exclusivity and luxury to their members.

The Indus Club in Mumbai has a secret entrance that members can use for confidential meetings and to conduct important business deals. The club, which opened in June 2017, caters to high-achieving individuals in fields such as film, media, sports, and business. It offers a range of amenities, including meeting rooms and event spaces, as well as a sense of privacy and discretion for its members. The club's promoter, Harish Thawani, commissioned a study before opening the club that found that potential members wanted exclusivity and discretion, and the club was designed with these features in mind. Sapna Keswani, the club's VP of Membership and Marketing, stated that the club aims to create a community of successful individuals and give them the freedom to do as they please.
